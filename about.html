Hello, I'm Navish

Paper is my
favourite canvas

It started with a single crane. I was around 12 years old, sitting in my grandfather's house in Mumbai, when he showed me how to fold a paper bird. That bird never flew — but something in me did.

Over the years, origami grew from a quiet hobby into a full creative practice. What began with cranes became dragons, phoenixes, entire safari collections. Each piece taught me something new about patience, geometry, and the strange alchemy of turning a flat square into a living form.

I started selling my work to friends, then strangers, and eventually launched OrigamiaaByNavish — a small studio right here in Mumbai where every single piece is handfolded by me. No machines. No shortcuts. Just paper, fingers, and a lot of love.

If you own one of my pieces, thank you. It means the world. ✦

— Navish

What I believe in

The philosophy of folding

🌿

Slow craft

In a world of fast everything, I believe in slow, intentional making. Each fold is deliberate. Each crease is a decision. There is no rush in this studio.

✋

Truly handmade

Every piece you see is folded by my hands — not printed, not mass-produced. If it says OrigamiaaByNavish, it was made by Navish. That's a promise.

🎁

Gifting with intention

Origami has been a gift between people for centuries. When you give one of my pieces, you're giving time, skill, and a little piece of someone's soul.

The journey

How it all unfolded

2015

The First Crane 🕊️

Learned origami from my grandfather. Folded my first crane — lopsided, but magical.

🕊️
📚
2017

Deep Dive 📚

Discovered complex origami — Robert Lang's tesselations, Yoshizawa's wetfolding. Spent hundreds of hours practising advanced forms.

2019

First Sale 🎉

A friend bought my dragon as a wedding gift for someone. That was the moment I realised this could be more than a hobby.

🎉
🚀
2023

OrigamiaaByNavish launches 🚀

Built the studio, created the brand, set up the shop. 50 designs, 200+ pieces sold. Still counting.
